Why this site?

This site is dedicated to support the systematic study of religion from the perspective of its oneness. Starting with the Holy Writings of Judaism, Christianity, Islam and the Bahá’í Faith, it will eventually cover all major religions.

In its methodological approach this site distinguishes on the one hand between the Holy Writings which the dherents of these Faiths believe to be of divine origin (Hebrew Bible, Gospel, Koran, Bahá’í Writings) and, on the other hand, the established commentaries or the religious traditions that arose from the former. The Writings are treated here as chapters from The Same Divine Book with the passages of one Scripture being explained through recourse to the Holy Text of the other faiths. As an example verses of the Koran are interpreted with the help of the verses of the New Testament using the Book of Certitude and vice versa. These interscriptural interpretations are compiled for key tenets upheld by the major religions such as life after death, heaven, hell, resurrection etc. They are presented in a generally understandable manner so that the reader can draw his or her own conclusions and evaluate the statements made by the religious traditions and the established commentaries. This is done by compiling the relevant data and facts from the Holy Writings and well established traditional commentaries as well as modern scholarly literature. This way those interested do not have to dig their way through the thicket of ancient commentary and modern scientific literature. They, for themselves, can get an impression of what exactly the One Common Faith underlying the great religious systems is and how it harmonizes with modern secular sciences. This way the websites seeks to make transparent the One Common Faith underlying the major world religions.

The idea of religions having something in common is not new. It is already mentioned in the Holy Writings, e.g. when Christ links his Faith directly to that of Moses (Joh 5:46) or the Koran (3:64) he uses the term Islam to designate both the Faith inaugurated by Muḥammad but also religion itself. The idea also has been already raised by leading scholars of the different religious backgrounds, like Rumi or most notably Clemens Alexandrinus who talks of the “One River of Truth” when referring to the common background of the Hebrew and Christian Scripture and the Greek philosophy. Modern scholars like Friedrich Heiler or Hans Küng also investigate commonalities of the major religious systems in their works. The difference of the approach taken here is, as set out in Bahá’u’lláhs Book of Certitude, to treat the Holy Writings as one Divine Scripture based on One Common Faith in contrast to man-made religious traditions and sects.

The Book of Certitude interprets countless passages of the Hebrew scriptures, the Gospel and the Koran to demonstrate the oneness of what we are taught to be different religions standing beside each other. According to the Book of Certitude what we see as different religions are reflections of One Single Reality or progressive revelations of One Common Divine Faith that is revealed in the course of history to an ever-evolving humanity. This happens by the appearance of “Manifestations of God” who are called prophets in most religious traditions. These Manifestations appear at different times, yet all represent the same, One God, reflect the same divine attributes, proclaim the same Faith and reveal the same Truth. Thus, it is an inadequate recognition of their station to see them as the founders of separate religions.

At its core this One Common Faith has eternal, at its periphery transitional features. The core constitutes “the changeless faith of God, eternal in the past, eternal in the future“ dealing with virtues like compassion, love, forgiveness, reconciliation. The transitory part consists of laws and ordinances that are formulated according to the requirements of the age and adapted in the next revelation once the old laws have fulfilled their purpose and the needs of humanity have changed. Then, clinging to the old laws often results in a ritualistic repetition of ordinances and practices long after these have lost their sense. The consequence is the trivialization of religion or its being identified with superstition, prejudice and backwardness resulting in skepticism and contempt towards religious organizations and movements as we see nowadays.

To sum up: If the major religions come from one source it must also be possible to interpret their Holy Writings as if they were chapters of one and the same book and come to conclusions that fit to all writings, modern secular sciences and - last but not least - general human common sense. This is done following the topics outlined in Bahá’ú’lláhs Book of Certitude. It is hoped that the view this sites offers on religion, contributes to eliminate the barriers between the different faiths thus facilitating their united participation “on eye level” in the global discourse with the secular world.
