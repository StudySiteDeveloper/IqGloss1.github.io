Why this site?

Recently, in an interfaith gathering in Germany, one of the Christian participants asked another guest with a different religious background: “So, is then my God also your God?”

In the current atmosphere of progressive globalization we are being exposed to different religious beliefs, the Writings of which claim to be from one God, the Supreme Being. In such an environment, despite all the differences between the religious systems, more and more people are asking themselves, whether these faiths come from the same one God or - from a secular viewpoint - from the same source. Nowadays, we all are more and more realizing that humanity does in fact constitute one people. So if there is only one God, and if there is one humanity, why are there so many different religions? Could it rather be that there is one religion lying just beneath the surface of the variuos religious systems? Could it be that what we call different religions are actually various facets of one source, of One Common Faith?

It is exactly these questions which are focus of this site that is dedicated to supporting the systematic study of religion from the perspective of Oneness by collecting data and facts from the Holy Wrtings, classical scholarly and modern scientifc literature. Starting with the Holy Writings of Judaism, Christianity, Islam and the Bahá’í Faith, it will eventually cover all major religions.

The approach is easily explained: If the major religions come from one source, if there is One Common Faith beneath them, it must be possible to interpret their Holy Writings as if they were chapters of one and the same book and come to conclusions that fit to all of them, to modern sciences and - last but not least - to common sense. By putting together such interpretations for key religious symbols and topics this site aims at demonstrating that the different religions have unfolded in the course of human history from One Common Faith from which.

It is hoped that the view this sites offers on religion, contributes to eliminating the barriers between the different faiths, thus facilitating their united participation “on eye level” in the global discourse with the secular world and contributing to finding solutions humanity is facing now.

Methodology

This site distinguishes on the one hand between the Holy Writings which the dherents of these Faiths believe to be of divine origin (Hebrew Bible, Gospel, Koran, Bahá’í Writings) and, on the other hand, the established commentaries or the religious traditions that arose from them. The Writings are treated here as chapters from The Same Divine Book with the passages of one Scripture being explained through recourse to the Holy Text of the other faiths. As an example verses of the Koran are interpreted with the help of the verses of the New Testament using the Book of Certitude and vice versa. These interscriptural interpretations are compiled for key tenets upheld by the major religions such as life after death, heaven, hell, resurrection, search for religious and scientific truth, etc. They are presented in a generally understandable manner so that the reader can draw his or her own conclusions and evaluate the statements made by the religious traditions and the established commentaries. This is done by compiling the relevant data and facts from the Holy Writings and well established traditional commentaries as well as modern scholarly literature. This way, those interested do not have to dig their way through the thicket of ancient commentary and modern scientific literature. The information is presented in a way that the readers, for themselves, can get an impression of what exactly the One Common Faith underlying the great religious systems is and how it harmonizes with modern secular sciences. This way the websites seeks to make transparent the One Common Faith underlying the major world religions.

The idea of religions having something in common is not new. It is already mentioned in the Holy Writings, e.g. when Christ links his Faith directly to that of Moses (Joh 5:46) or the Koran (3:64) using the term Islam to designate both the Faith inaugurated by Muḥammad but also religion itself. The idea also has been already raised by leading scholars of the different religious backgrounds, like Rumi or most notably Clemens Alexandrinus who talks of the “One River of Truth” when referring to the common background of the Hebrew and Christian Scripture and the Greek philosophy. Modern scholars like Friedrich Heiler or Hans Küng also investigated and demonstrated commonalities of the major religious systems in their works. The difference of the approach taken here is, as set out in Bahá’u’lláhs Book of Certitude, to treat the Holy Writings as one Divine Scripture based on One Common Faith in contrast to man-made religious traditions.

The Book of Certitude interprets countless passages of the Hebrew scriptures, the Gospel and the Koran to demonstrate the oneness of what we are taught to be different religions standing beside each other. According to the Book of Certitude what we see as different religions are reflections of One Single Reality or progressive revelations of One Common Divine Faith that is revealed in the course of history to an ever-evolving humanity. This happens by the appearance of “Manifestations of God” who are called prophets in most religious traditions. These Manifestations appear at different times, yet all represent the same, One God, reflect the same divine attributes, proclaim the same Faith and reveal the same Truth. Thus, it is an inadequate recognition of their station to see them as the founders of separate religions.

At its core this One Common Faith has eternal, at its periphery transitional features. The core constitutes of “the changeless faith of God, eternal in the past, eternal in the future“ dealing with virtues like compassion, love, forgiveness, reconciliation. The transitory part consists of laws and ordinances that are formulated according to the requirements of the age and adapted in the next revelation once the old laws have fulfilled their purpose and the needs of humanity have changed. Then, clinging to the old laws often results in a ritualistic repetition of ordinances and practices long after these have lost their sense. The consequence is the trivialization of religion or its being identified with superstition, prejudice and backwardness resulting in skepticism and contempt towards religious organizations and movements as we see nowadays.

Using this briefly sketched methodological approach this site will demonstrate that what we see as different religions are in fact stages in the limitless unfolding of a single Reality.
